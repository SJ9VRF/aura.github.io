Flagship Research Themes

Multimodal Foundation Models

Research on generalizable multimodal foundation models spanning 3D, vision, language, and action, with emphasis on real-time perception, dynamic interaction, world modeling, robotic control, and scalable learning from large offline datasets.

This work is grounded in building systems that move beyond static prediction toward embodied reasoning, perception, and action in environments that demand generalization and reliability.

Alignment

Research spanning multimodal alignment, reflective self-play, communication-driven capability shaping, and safer deployment of advanced AI systems. Focus areas include failure-mode analysis, robustness, steerability, oversight, and evaluation of misalignment risk under real-world conditions.

This includes work relevant to high-stakes settings where reliability, interpretability, cultural sensitivity, and structured feedback matter for making powerful models more governable and trustworthy.

Autonomy and Agents

Designed and built agentic systems operating across multimodal inputs with capabilities in reasoning, planning, tool use, memory, retrieval, and long-horizon task execution. Work includes adaptive agent frameworks, structured reasoning, OpenAPI-based tool orchestration, and software interaction.

These systems are aimed at complex workflows where models must act over multiple steps, use external tools, navigate interfaces, and maintain reliable decision structure over time.

Embodied AI and Robotics

Work across robot learning, manipulation, causal learning, failure detection, sim-to-real transfer, and action-aligned perception and control. Research includes long-horizon assembly, multimodal task representation, and reinforcement learning for robotic behavior.

The emphasis is on systems that combine language, vision, 3D, and action to support more adaptive physical intelligence.
